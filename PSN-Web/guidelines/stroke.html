Introduction

These guidelines provide evidence-based recommendations for the diagnosis, management, and prevention of stroke in Pakistan. They are designed to assist healthcare providers in making informed decisions about stroke care while considering local resources and circumstances.

Important Update: These guidelines were last updated in March 2025 and incorporate the latest evidence and recommendations from international stroke organizations, adapted for the Pakistani healthcare context.

Diagnosis

Initial Assessment

  • Use BE-FAST criteria for stroke recognition:
    • Balance - Sudden loss of balance
    • Eyes - Sudden vision problems
    • Face - Facial drooping
    • Arm - Arm weakness
    • Speech - Speech difficulty
    • Time - Time to call emergency services

Essential Investigations

Investigation Timing Purpose
Non-contrast CT Brain Immediate Rule out hemorrhage, early ischemic changes
Blood Tests Within 45 minutes CBC, Coagulation profile, Blood sugar, Electrolytes
ECG Within 60 minutes Identify AF and other cardiac conditions

Acute Management

Golden Hour: The first hour after stroke onset is critical. Implement these measures immediately.

Initial Steps

  1. Ensure airway, breathing, circulation
  2. Check blood glucose
  3. Obtain IV access
  4. Monitor vital signs
  5. Complete NIHSS assessment

Blood Pressure Management

Stroke Type BP Target Medications
Ischemic Stroke ≤ 185/110 mmHg (if eligible for thrombolysis) Labetalol, Nicardipine
Hemorrhagic Stroke SBP < 140 mmHg Labetalol, Nicardipine, Clevidipine

Thrombolysis

Inclusion Criteria

  • Age ≥ 18 years
  • Diagnosis of ischemic stroke causing measurable neurological deficit
  • Time of onset < 4.5 hours

Absolute Contraindications

  • Significant head trauma or stroke in previous 3 months
  • Symptoms suggestive of SAH
  • Arterial puncture at noncompressible site in previous 7 days
  • History of previous ICH
  • Intracranial neoplasm, AVM, or aneurysm
  • Recent intracranial or intraspinal surgery
  • Active internal bleeding
Critical Note: Thrombolysis should only be administered in centers with appropriate expertise and infrastructure for stroke care.

Prevention

Primary Prevention

Modifiable Risk Factors
  • Hypertension
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Atrial fibrillation
  • Smoking
  • Physical inactivity
  • Obesity
Recommendations
  • Regular blood pressure monitoring
  • Diabetes control
  • Smoking cessation
  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy diet
  • Weight management

Secondary Prevention

Intervention Recommendation
Antiplatelet Therapy Aspirin 75-150mg daily
Anticoagulation For AF: DOACs or Warfarin
Blood Pressure Target < 130/80 mmHg
Lipids High-intensity statin therapy

Rehabilitation

Early Rehabilitation

  • Start within 24-48 hours if medically stable
  • Initial focus on preventing complications
  • Gradually increase intensity based on patient tolerance

Multidisciplinary Team

Physical Therapy
  • Mobility training
  • Balance exercises
  • Strength training
Occupational Therapy
  • ADL training
  • Home adaptation
  • Upper limb therapy
Speech Therapy
  • Speech exercises
  • Swallowing assessment
  • Communication aids
