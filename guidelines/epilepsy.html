Diagnosis

Clinical Assessment

  • Detailed history taking
  • Physical and neurological examination
  • Seizure classification
  • Family history assessment

Diagnostic Tests

  • EEG (routine and sleep-deprived)
  • MRI brain (with epilepsy protocol)
  • Basic blood work
  • Video EEG monitoring when indicated

Treatment

First-line Medications

Medication Initial Dose Maintenance Dose Primary Indication
Carbamazepine 200mg/day 400-1200mg/day Focal seizures
Levetiracetam 500mg/day 1000-3000mg/day Broad spectrum
Sodium Valproate 500mg/day 1000-2000mg/day Generalized seizures

Treatment Strategy

  1. Start with monotherapy
  2. Titrate to effective dose
  3. Monitor for side effects
  4. Consider alternative/add-on therapy if inadequate response

Monitoring

Follow-up Schedule

  • Initial follow-up: 2-4 weeks
  • Dose adjustment period: Monthly
  • Stable patients: Every 3-6 months
  • Annual comprehensive review

Monitoring Parameters

  • Seizure frequency and severity
  • Medication adherence
  • Side effects
  • Quality of life measures

Special Populations

Women of Childbearing Age

  • Pre-conception counseling
  • Folic acid supplementation
  • Medication adjustments
  • Pregnancy monitoring

Elderly Patients

  • Consider comorbidities
  • Drug interactions
  • Lower starting doses
  • Regular monitoring

Pediatric Patients

  • Age-specific considerations
  • Growth and development monitoring
  • School performance assessment
  • Family support systems
